Are you feeling the weight of what's happening in the world right now?

The doom that peaks with every new headline, and then peaks again. The sense that something fundamental is shifting — in the economy, in politics, in the climate, in the fabric of things you thought you could count on.

The building tension that follows you into the morning and sits with you at night. The exhaustion of trying to stay informed without being overwhelmed, to stay hopeful without feeling naive, to keep functioning when the ground beneath you feels increasingly unstable.

If any of this sounds familiar, you are not alone — and you are not broken.

What you are experiencing is a nervous system doing exactly what nervous systems are designed to do: scanning for threat, preparing for impact, sounding the alarm. The problem is that this alarm was built for threats that come and go — not for the kind of relentless ambient uncertainty that defines life in 2026.

When the threat doesn't resolve, the alarm doesn't switch off. And that comes at a high cost. It costs your sleep, your concentration, your relationships, your ability to feel joy or rest or the simple pleasure of being alive in your own life.

This retreat is for people who are tired of paying that cost — and who are ready to do something about it.

What This Retreat Is

A stable inner reference point

Finding Stillness in the Storm is a three-day immersive retreat designed to give you what chronic uncertainty strips away: a stable inner reference point. Not a temporary escape from the world, but a real, practised capacity to meet it — without being flattened by it.

Over three days, you will work with the body, the breath, and the mind in an integrated way, drawing on two complementary sources of knowledge: a 5,000-year-old contemplative tradition that has been helping human beings navigate inner turbulence, and contemporary neuroscience and clinical psychology that can now explain, in precise physiological terms, why it works.

You will leave not with a weekend of peace that evaporates on the ride home, but with a set of tools, practices, and — perhaps most importantly — a felt, embodied sense of what it means to be steady. Something you can return to when the next wave comes.

"Not a temporary escape from the world, but a real, practised capacity to meet it — without being flattened by it." — The retreat's founding intention

What You Will Experience

Five pillars of practice

01
The Body as an Anchor
Through carefully sequenced yoga practices rooted in the Bihar tradition — one of the world's oldest and most rigorous living yoga lineages — you will learn to use your own body as a regulation tool. Sessions combine extended asana practice, pranayama (yogic breathwork), and yoga nidra: a form of guided conscious deep rest that research has shown to shift the nervous system out of chronic activation states more effectively than ordinary sleep.

These are not nice stretches with ambient music. They are precise, intelligent practices, taught by someone who has spent twenty years living and training within the tradition that created them.
+
02
The Mind as an Ally
Understanding what is happening inside you changes your relationship to it. In dedicated sessions drawing on cognitive neuroscience, clinical psychology, and the yogic map of the mind, you will learn why your nervous system responds to uncertainty the way it does — and why that response, however exhausting, is not a sign that something is wrong with you.

You will be introduced to the pancha koshas — the yogic model of the human being as five interconnected layers — and discover how remarkably well it maps onto what contemporary psychology and neuroscience are only now beginning to articulate.
+
03
Connection as Medicine
We were never meant to process any of this alone. This retreat includes facilitated authentic relating sessions — structured practices in real, present-moment connection — that offer something increasingly rare: the experience of being fully met by other human beings, without performance or pretence.

These sessions are carefully held, deeply accessible regardless of prior experience, and consistently named by participants as among the most powerful elements of any retreat. We regulate each other. Not as a metaphor — as a biological fact.
+
04
Clarity About What Matters
When the external world is chaotic, it becomes essential — not optional — to know what you actually stand for. What you value. What you are unwilling to lose. What you are here to give.

The SWAN practice — a structured yogic self-inquiry into your Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Needs — offers a rare and honest taking-stock that cuts through the noise and returns you to your own centre of gravity.
+
05
Nourishment, Nature & Shared Life
There will be walks in nature. There will be good food. One evening you will cook together — momos and dahl bhat, a Nepali meal, made by hand in a group — because there is something that happens when people prepare food together that no workshop can replicate.

There will be kirtan — group chanting — for those drawn to it, and honest evening conversation for those who prefer ideas to music. There will be more silence than you are used to, and you will be surprised by how much you needed it.
+

Is This Retreat For You?

A small, serious, warm gathering

This retreat is for you if
  • You are feeling the weight of global uncertainty, and it is affecting your daily life in ways you can no longer ignore
  • You are curious about yoga or meditation but put off by retreats that feel either too physical or too vaguely spiritual
  • You are a mental health professional, therapist, or counsellor who wants to explore the intersection of contemplative practice and clinical psychology — from the inside
  • You are a yoga teacher or practitioner looking for serious content that deepens both your personal practice and your ability to support students who are struggling
  • You want to understand — not just experience, but genuinely understand — why these practices work, and how to keep using them when you get home
  • You are ready to spend three days in honest company, doing real work, in a beautiful place
This retreat is not for you if
  • You are looking for a spa weekend
  • You want a festival atmosphere
  • You are seeking a certification course

It is a small, serious, warm gathering of people doing genuine inner work together.

For nearly two decades she worked in responsible sourcing in a large corporation — and then the yoga practice quietly shifted her sense of what mattered. She is now a licensed clinical psychologist (under supervision) in Romania, with a specialisation in psycho-oncology and ongoing training in cognitive-behavioural psychotherapy.

What We Are Not Promising

We are not promising that three days will resolve what the world is currently doing. We are not promising enlightenment, transformation, or that you will leave as a different person.

We are promising this: a small group of real people, two facilitators who have been living these practices long enough to teach them honestly, a beautiful and quiet place, and three days of serious, warm, genuinely useful work together.

The kind of work that gives you something to hold onto when you need it. That, in the current moment, feels like enough.
